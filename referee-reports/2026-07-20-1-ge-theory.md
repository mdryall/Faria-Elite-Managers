# Referee Pass 1: General Equilibrium Theory (verified against the EGSZ chapter and companion scripts)

*Adversarial referee simulation, 2026-07-20. Status annotations added after the repair pass: [FIXED] = repaired in commit df461f6; [DEFERRED] = open item.*

## Findings

**1. MAJOR — Uniqueness clause of Lemma 1 (characterization) is not proven as stated.** [FIXED]
The wage equations are derived from indifference *with leisure*. "All six memberships active" does not imply a positive measure of agents chooses the empty list. If σ₀ = 0, all non-elite occupations need only yield a *common* utility u₀ ≥ k, and the price system is not forced. Assumption (iv) permitted σ₀ = 0. Fix: strengthen (iv) to σ₀ > 0 and condition the uniqueness clause on a positive leisure mass.

**2. MAJOR — Proposition 3 (positional), part (ii) is false as stated.** [FIXED]
The claim: any FOSD shift of F raises m\* by strictly less than it raises m₀. But the relevant vertical shifts are evaluated at *different points* (the positional cutoff η₀\*/v(m\*) exceeds η₀\*). Counterexample: a FOSD shift modifying F only in a neighborhood of the positional cutoff gives dm₀ = 0 but dm\* > 0, reversing the inequality. The dm\* = dρ/(1+|∂ρ/∂m|) equation is also a differential identity applied to a discrete shift. The numerical claim is correct for the exponential scale family but does not generalize. Fix: restate with an explicit shift-location hypothesis.

**3. MAJOR — Proposition 4 (inefficiency): the "movers at reservation utility" step is wrong for any dm > 0.** [FIXED]
Shutting a positive mass dm of elite triples moves managers with η ∈ (η\*, η_dm) to leisure; every such agent except the marginal type has strictly positive status surplus and is made strictly worse off. The proof never compensates them. The argument is rescuable — aggregate mover losses are O(dm²) while inframarginal de-dilution gains are O(dm) — but must be written out. Also unstated: (a) a_e = ½(τr+βc+αp₂φ) > 0 and a_c = ½(c+r+p₂φ) > 0 (verified symbolically), which guarantees dn > 0; (b) staffing dn new competent triples requires σ₀ > 0 (in the example dn/dm ≈ 1.45 > 1). The collinearity/exact-feasibility core is otherwise airtight.

**4. MAJOR (borderline FATAL for the stated proof) — Proposition 5 (membership tax), part (ii): the key step is contradicted by the paper's own example.** [FIXED]
"At t=0 every private margin is at a first-order optimum, so the derivative of utilitarian welfare reduces to the uninternalized de-dilution term" — false. Computed at the paper's parameters: W′(0) = 0.00125 while the de-dilution term = 0.00058, a factor of 2.15. The tax-plus-rebate is not an envelope perturbation: the rebate shifts every agent's effective endowment (moving all wages, p₂, and z at first order) and redistributes between agents with unequal marginal utilities of income. Consequences: (i) "welfare strictly increasing at t=0" is verified only in the example; (ii) the identification of the "first-order Pigouvian level" with the wedge is incorrect — t^W = 0.312 ≠ wedge = 0.347 is a conceptual, not numerical, discrepancy (the wedge is a planner-family FOC quantity). Part (i)'s uniqueness argument also ignored the joint (m, T) fixed point.

**5. MAJOR — Proposition 6 (transition map), part (iii): stability is asserted, not proven.** [FIXED: weakened]
A crossing from above gives ξ′ ≤ 1 but not ξ′ > −1, and ξ is not shown monotone. "A stable interior steady state exists" is established only for the example. Additionally, the appendix's ξ′ = 0.31 matches nothing in the script's output (absolute: 0.174) — a stale number contradicting the "machine-verified" advertising.

**6. MINOR — FOSD strictness non sequitur.** [FIXED]
Strict monotonicity of F does not imply the shift is strict at the cutoff. Admissible shifts now defined with strictness at the relevant cutoff.

**7. MINOR — Proposition 2 proof: factor-of-2 slip and unbacked "machine-verified" tag.** [FIXED]
Verified symbolically: dȳ/dm\* = φ[α(c+r) − (βc+τr)] / [2c+2r+(μ+2λ−3)k]. The text's "over that denominator" was off by a factor of 2 (sign unaffected). The static script contained no check of this identity despite the tag. Both fixed; check added.

**8. MINOR — Strict monotonicity fails on the boundary; EGSZ's maintained assumption technically fails.** [FIXED: remark added]
With h = √(x₁x₂), utility is constant along the axes. Under the strong reading of "strictly monotone" the maintained assumption fails; the core/FWT argument requires only local nonsatiation, which holds everywhere. The desirable-endowments verification itself is **correct** against EGSZ's definition.

**9. MINOR — Proposition 3 uniqueness argument imprecise for bounded support.** [FIXED]
ρ is not strictly decreasing where it vanishes (bounded support); uniqueness still holds via ρ(m) − m strictly decreasing.

**10. MINOR — Missing assumptions for the wedge.** [FIXED]
v′ and a density of F were used without assuming v ∈ C¹, differentiability of F, or a finite mean. All added; density written F′ to avoid collision with the organizational characteristic f.

**11. MINOR — No definition of dynamic equilibrium; interiority along the path unassumed.** [FIXED]
Definition added (recursive, with period-equilibrium selection); domain interval K stated. Note: uniform inheritance **is** aggregate-consistent — ∫(1−ζ)w_a dι / p₂ = (1−ζ)w̄/p₂ — verified, no error there.

**12. MINOR — Steady-state comparisons assume an unproven unique/orderable crossing.** [FIXED: restated for corresponding crossings, uniqueness noted as example fact]

**13. MINOR — Framework statement: small omissions.** [FIXED]
(a) Measurability conditions compressed (integrability of endowments omitted). (b) Feasibility-for-a-coalition never defined, though the core is invoked. (c) The strong-Pareto gloss mis-rendered the negation. All three fixed.

**14. MINOR — "Machine-verified" overreach.** [FIXED]
Missing checks added (Prop-2 identity; global drag identity replacing 4-point check); title footnote now states precisely what the code verifies. All *numerical* claims cross-checked in Sections 5–7 matched the scripts exactly.

## Things attacked and found correct (do not "fix")

The desirable-endowments verification against EGSZ's actual definition is valid. The statements of group types, memberships, lists, states, consistency, the three equilibrium conditions, and Theorems 1–2 match the chapter. All static equilibrium algebra — prices, p₂, the z/cutoff equation, market clearing, Walras, the compensation decomposition, the coexistence identity, and the numerical example in both regimes — is correct and reproduced by the scripts. The luxury identity and drag sign identity are correct (verified symbolically, the latter globally). Uniform inheritance is aggregate-consistent. The positional equilibrium concept is coherent, and the value-neutrality/collinearity core of the Proposition 4 construction is sound.

## Overall assessment (as delivered, pre-repair)

The paper's baseline (Sections 3–5) is formally solid. The trouble is concentrated where the paper goes beyond the inherited machinery: Propositions 3(ii), 4, 5(ii), and 6(iii) require re-derivation, not wordsmithing. None appears unfixable — the model is tractable enough that every gap can be closed in closed form.
